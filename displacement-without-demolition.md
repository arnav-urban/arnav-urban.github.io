## Introduction
For decades, Indian courts have played a central role in shaping urban land, housing, and informality.  
While demolitions attract public attention, a quieter process of displacement has unfolded through legality itself.

This essay examines how judicial reasoning has gradually redefined urban citizenship, ownership, and legitimacyâ€”often without physical eviction.

---

## Core Argument
Rather than acting only as protectors or violators of rights, courts have increasingly facilitated market-oriented urban governance.  
Through doctrines of legality, planned development, and public interest, informal settlements are rendered vulnerable without explicit demolition.

---

## Why This Matters for Planning
- Displacement is increasingly **institutional**, not physical  
- Planning law acts as a **filter of legitimacy**  
- Courts shape urban form as much as master plans
